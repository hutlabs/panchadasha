Fire Fighting Robot (11-13 January)

What if robots replace fire department people. Sounds good right. Want to design one. Here is a golden opportunity Panchadasha is hosting a workshop on how to build a fire fighting robot. Highly experienced professionals from HUTLAB are undertaking this workshop. The workshop is about understanding the functioning of microcontroller and some senors. You will practically learn how to interface microcontroller with Android phone via Bluetooth interface. Finally the participants should design a prototype of bluetooth interfaced fire fighting robot attached with some sensors.

There is a competition associated with this workshop, Fire Fighting Robot Competition. Make sure you register for that too.

Conducted by:

  • Dr. Rajesh Kannan M (HuT Labs)
  • Mr. Sreekanth M.M (HuT Labs)

Neural Networks (13 January)

This workshop is for all the people who are intersted in Neuromodulation and the various techniques used in it. It gives you an unique opportunity to meet with researchers in this field from the industries. Neuromodulation is the alteration of nerve activity through targeted delivery of stimulus. Various techniques such as fMRI and TMS associated with neuromodulation would be dealt with in details. People interested in signal processing can see this as a wonderful opportunities to enchance their capabilites.

Cloud Computing (13 January)

The world is moving into an increasingly server-less model to enable cost effective, scale-able computing. In this session you will be introduced to the fundamentals of building server less cloud architectures for connected embedded systems like IoT sensor networks and voice controlled gadgets. This will include networking basics, process of connecting a regular device into a global network and accessing devices from anywhere in the world.

Machine Learning (13 January)

Want to add Artificial Intellegence(AI) for your program, Machine Learning is an application of Artificial Intelligence (AI) that provides systems the ability to automatically learn and improve from experience without being explicitly programmed.
