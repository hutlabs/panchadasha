Speakers

Dr. Rajesh Kannan M

Asst. Professor, Dept. of ECE, Amrita Vishwa Vidyapeetham.

Dr. Rajesh Kannan Megalingam is an electronics engineer leading research on humanitarian technologies with special emphasis on Robotics at HuT (Humanitarian Technology) Labs. His research areas include Embedded Systems, Robotics, Semiconductors, and Healthcare. His research focuses on autonomous robots, robots for rehabilitation, and VLSI etc. He has worked as VLSI Design and Verification Engineer at various companies like STMicro Electronics, Insilicon Incorporation etc. in Bay Area, California, USA, for 6 years.

Mr. Vysakh Pillai

Principal Engineer, Microchip Technology.

Vysakh Pillai has close to 10 years of experience in the embedded systems industry. Having worked with major players including VSSC, Nokia, and Cisco in the past, he has a wide range of expertise in the full stack of embedded device development from hardware design to cloud connectivity architectures. He completed undergraduate studies at Amrita University and graduate studies at Manipal University.

Mr. Ajithesh Gupta

Software Development Engineer, Microsoft, Hyderabad.

Ajithesh Gupta completed his graduation in ECE from Amrita Vishwa Vidyapeetham, Kerala and later worked with CERNER HealthCare Solutions. He was an active member of HuT Labs and published IEEE papers during his BTech. He is a full stack developer with 5 years of experience in Software Industry working in .Net technologies, SQL, Azure, C#, Machine Learning, Angular etc.

Mr. Shree Rajesh Raagul Vadivel

Robotic Engineer, HuT Labs.

Mr. Rajesh is a graduate in B.E. in EEE from S.A. Engineering college, Affiliated to Anna university Chennai, Tamilnadu. He previously worked as an Application Engineer in Sixth Sense Robotics and Automation PVT LTD. He has done a commendable research in the following areas, Application design, Control panel designing and making, Industrial PC based robot mastering and programming.

Mr. Sreekanth M.M

PhD Scholar, HuT Labs.

Mr. Sreekanth M.M has completed his B.Tech in Electronics and Communication from Sahrdaya College of Engineering and Technology, Thrissur, Kerala, India and his masters in VLSI Design at Amrita School of Engineering Kollam, Kerala, India. He is currently doing PhD and also Working as Research Associate at Humanitarian and Technology lab, Amrita University, Kollam, Kerala, India. His area of interests include Robotics, Embedded System and Haptics.

Mr. Sakthiprasad K.M

PhD Scholar, HuT Labs.

Mr. Sakthiprasad K.M received the M.Tech degree in Applied Electronics and Communication Systems from Thejus Engineering College, Kerala, India. He is currently a PhD candidate in the Humanitarian Technology (HuT) Labs. His Ph D. thesis focuses on Object recognition, identification & depth estimation on a 3D plane involving non-deterministic structures. His area of interests are Robotics, Computer Vision, Image Processing, and Power Efficient Solar Energy Systems.

Mr. Muni Karthik

Microsoft, Bengaluru.

Muni Karthik graduation in ECE from Amrita School of Engineering, Kerala and holds a PG certificate in Big Data Analytics and Optimization from CMU. He then worked in Infosys as Software engineer in Jan 2015. Later he moved to United Health Group (UHG) as Senior Systems Engineer. Currently, he is working with Microsoft, Bengaluru on projects related to Machine Learning and few other technologies.

Mr. Praveen James

Engineer, SCTIMST, Trivandrum.

Mr. Praveen James has completed his B. E in Biomedical Engineering in Rajiv Gandhi college of Engineering, Chennai. Later he has worked as a Biomedical Engineer in FLL, International Centre of cardiovascular diseases. He has published many international publications and has been a part of many Scientific Projects. His field of interest lies in Bio-Medical Engineering.

Mr. Vineeth Radhakrishnan

Research Fellow, SCTIMST, Trivandrum.

Mr. Vineeth Radhakrishnan is a Biomedical engineering graduate with experience in medical imaging and controls systems engineering. He is currently working as research fellow on study of brain connectivity in Parkinson’s Disease. He is experienced in working at Small Animal Imaging Facility, at Van Andel Research Institute utilizing imaging modalities including µSPECT/CT, PET and Ultrasound.
